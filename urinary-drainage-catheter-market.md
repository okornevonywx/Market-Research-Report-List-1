<p><p>The urinary drainage catheter market comprises three main types: rubber, plastic (PVC), and silicone. Rubber catheters offer flexibility and durability but may be less biocompatible. PVC catheters are lightweight and cost-effective but can cause irritation. Silicone catheters are known for their high biocompatibility, reducing the risk of infection and irritation, making them suitable for long-term use. Each type has distinct advantages and disadvantages, impacting their selection based on clinical needs and patient comfort.</p></p>
